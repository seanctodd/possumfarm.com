17 Possum Facts That Will Change Your Entire Life

factseducationpossums are greatpapi speaks

Every day, people walk around not knowing enough about possums. This is a crime against knowledge. I, Papi Possum, am here to fix this.

Here are 17 possum facts that I personally guarantee will change your life, your perspective, and possibly your career trajectory.

1. Possums are the only marsupials native to North America.

That means while Australia is out there bragging about kangaroos and koalas, America has been sitting on this incredible marsupial the entire time and nobody told anybody. Shameful.

2. They have 50 teeth.

  1. Fifty. That’s more teeth than any other North American land mammal. Do they need all 50? Unknown. Do they have them anyway? Absolutely yes.

3. They are immune to most snake venoms.

The Virginia opossum produces a peptide in its blood called LTNF (Lethal Toxin-Neutralizing Factor) that neutralizes snake venom. Scientists are studying this for potential human applications. Possums have been doing medicine this whole time and never once bragged about it.

4. “Playing dead” is called thanatosis, and they can’t control it.

When threatened, possums enter an involuntary catatonic state that can last minutes or hours. They fall over, go limp, their heart rate drops, and they even produce a smell like rotting flesh. It happens automatically — the possum does not decide to do this. Their body just goes “okay we’re doing the death thing now.” Papi finds this deeply relatable.

5. Their body temperature is too low for most diseases.

Including rabies. Possums almost never carry rabies. They are statistically safer than your average raccoon, squirrel, or coworker.

6. They eat ticks. Thousands of them.

A single possum eats approximately 5,000 ticks per season. They are nature’s Roomba for Lyme disease vectors. Every possum on my farm is actively protecting the surrounding neighborhood and not once have the neighbors sent a thank-you card. Rude.

7. Baby possums are called joeys.

Just like kangaroos. They are born at 13 days gestation, smaller than a honeybee, and they crawl into their mother’s pouch on instinct alone. Papi cried when he learned this.

8. Possums have opposable thumbs on their hind feet.

These are called hallux and they use them for gripping branches. They are better climbers than you. This is simply true.

9. Their prehensile tails can grip things.

Young possums can hang from their tails. Adults mostly use their tails for balance and carrying nesting materials. The whole hanging-upside-down thing is a bit of an exaggeration. They can do it, they just don’t prefer it. Papi also doesn’t hang upside down unless absolutely necessary.

10. They are mostly solitary and non-aggressive.

Possums don’t want drama. They want grubs, fruit, and a quiet place to sleep. They are nature’s introverts.

11. They groom themselves constantly.

Like cats. They are extremely clean animals. The hissing and scary face they make when cornered is entirely a bluff. Behind the hiss is a very anxious creature that just wants everyone to back off.

12. Their lifespan is typically 2–4 years in the wild.

This is sad. Bernadette, my first possum, is 21. I attribute this to: love, good nutrition, and the fact that she has never encountered a car or a predator because she lives on my farm in a custom enclosure with heated floors that cost me $3,000 and I would do it again.

13. They appeared 70 million years ago.

Possums are older than most mountains. They survived the asteroid that killed the dinosaurs. They are ancient and wise and they are living in your neighborhood right now. Show some respect.

14. They are beneficial to your garden.

Possums eat slugs, snails, beetles, and other garden pests. They are free pest control. If you see a possum in your yard, you should leave it a thank-you note and some grapes.

15. Possums produce their own vitamin C.

Like most mammals except humans and guinea pigs, possums synthesize their own vitamin C internally. They never get scurvy. Papi takes vitamin C supplements and is slightly jealous.

16. They cannot carry leptospirosis.

Another disease they’re immune to. At this point I think possums are just showing off.

17. They are generally misunderstood.

People see a possum and think: scary, gross, rat-like. But possums are none of these things. They are gentle, ancient, immune to disease, doing incredible ecological work, and they have been on this earth for 70 million years without asking anyone for anything.

They deserve better.

That’s what Possum Farm is about.
