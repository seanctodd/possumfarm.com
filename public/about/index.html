About Papi Possum

The Origin Story

Nobody really knows where Papi Possum came from. Some say he emerged from the woods one foggy morning in 2003, holding a baby possum in each hand and a half-eaten breakfast burrito in his mouth. Others say he was born on the farm itself, which would explain a lot.

What we do know is this: Papi Possum loves possums with his whole heart, and he has dedicated his life to giving these misunderstood marsupials the five-star treatment they deserve.

The Philosophy

Papi runs Possum Farm on three core principles:

  1. Every possum deserves dignity. Even the weird ones. Especially the weird ones.
  2. Play dead when necessary. This applies to both possums and farmers facing difficult conversations.
  3. The nocturnal life is the good life. Most of Papi’s best decisions were made between 2am and 4am.

The Farm

Possum Farm spans 40 acres of lush, organic, mostly-unmowed terrain on the outskirts of a small town that asked to remain anonymous (it’s the possums, they’re nervous). The land features:

  • The Great Slumber Meadow — a 10-acre expanse where possums nap in peace
  • The Enrichment Zone — an obstacle course made entirely of cardboard boxes and fallen logs
  • The Snack Barn — stocked with the finest overripe persimmons, grubs, and what Papi calls “the good garbage”
  • Papi’s Cottage — a converted tool shed that Papi insists is “rustic, not sad”
