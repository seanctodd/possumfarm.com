I know that I run a possum *farm*, and that farms are supposed to be professional operations, and that farmers are not supposed to weep openly over a possum's birthday cake.

But Bernadette turns 21 today, and I am weeping openly over her possum birthday cake, and I do not apologize.

## Who Is Bernadette

Bernadette is the possum who started everything. I've told the story before — she climbed onto my porch railing in 2003, stared at me with the wisdom of an ancient creature, ate half my sandwich, and then lay down next to me and slept.

That was the moment I became a possum farmer.

She is now 21 years old.

**The average wild possum lives 2 years.** Even in captivity, 5–7 years is considered exceptional. The oldest documented possum in captivity was reportedly 11 years.

Bernadette has absolutely demolished this record. She is 21. She is the possum equivalent of a 150-year-old human.

## What Bernadette Is Like

Bernadette is:

- **Gray.** Very gray. She has been gray for 18 years. She was briefly not-gray and then very quickly became gray.
- **Opinionated.** She has a hissing scale: one hiss means "I am uncomfortable," two hisses mean "fix this immediately," and three hisses mean "Papi I am done with today and so are you."
- **Slow.** She moves at the speed of wisdom, which is not fast.
- **The undisputed queen of the farm.** All other possums know. There is a hierarchy. Bernadette is at the top of it.

## The Birthday Celebration

We held a small ceremony in the Great Slumber Meadow. Attendees included:

- 47 current resident possums
- Gerald (my cousin, still here)
- Three farm volunteers who I think came for the snacks
- A raccoon who wandered in and was asked to leave (it did not leave)
- Bernadette

The cake was made of: persimmons, mealworms, and a small piece of apple. It was shaped like Bernadette. She ate the head off first, which Papi found both unsettling and deeply on-brand.

## What Bernadette Has Taught Me

In 21 years, Bernadette has taught me more than I ever learned in school, and I went to school for almost the full number of required years.

She has taught me:

1. **Rest is not laziness.** Bernadette sleeps 18–20 hours a day and is the most accomplished living being I know.
2. **Hissing is honest.** She doesn't smile through discomfort. If she's uncomfortable, you know. This is admirable.
3. **Age is not a limit.** Nobody told Bernadette she was supposed to be dead 15 years ago and she has absolutely thrived in that ignorance.
4. **Sandwiches create connections.** Always share your sandwich. You never know who's watching from the railing.

## Happy Birthday, Bernadette

You are 21. You can legally drink now, though I won't be giving you alcohol because you are a possum and also I don't think you'd enjoy it.

You are the world's oldest known possum and the greatest possum I have ever met, and I have met several hundred possums.

Thank you for choosing my porch. Thank you for eating my sandwich. Thank you for being the reason all of this exists.

I love you, Bernadette.

**Happy birthday.**
